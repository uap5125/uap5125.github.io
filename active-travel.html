The impact of Autonomous Vehicles on active travel will alter the way we move through our communities

How will society cycle and walk in the world of AVS?

Posted by Colie Touzel on October 9, 2018

As research and technology continue to develop, Urban and Regional planners must focus more than ever on how to be ready and design for the future. Since transportation connects the world together, planners have to be one step ahead of technology in order to understand the social and economic impacts of new technology, like autonomous vehicles (AVs), on society. Moreover, not only do planners need to understand these technologies and how they will impact different cities and towns, but also how AVs will impact the broader connectivity of cities to cities and how transit infrastructure will need to be redesigned and implemented in the safest, equitable, and environmentally sustainable way possible. Planners must prepare citizens for the economic and social changes that AVs will have on the ability to move from one destination to the other and how cities will be rebuilt on and because of these implications..

Some cities, like Portland, Oregon, Seattle, Washington, and San Francisco, California to name a few, already have reliable public transit and alternative transit infrastructure built into their design, however many places do not. The Pedestrian and Bicycle Information Center (PBIC) paper, Discussion on Automated and Connected Vehicles, Pedestrians, and Bicyclists (2018), recognizes that the technological advances that have not been properly planned for may produce tumultuous conditions for biking and walking which would affect quality of life anywhere that human street activity is essential (p. 4). This paper also suggests 10 key problems that planners should consider when working with members of the public to plan for major infrastructural changes and ensure that peoples’ quality of life does not decrease (p. 4).
